Why an app

Therapy is out of reach for most people.

CBT works. Getting to it is the hard part — and that gap is exactly what keeps people stuck in the loop instead of working through it.

Wait

Waitlists run for months

By the time an appointment comes up, the motivation or crisis that pushed you to book it has often already passed.

Cost

Sessions add up fast

Weekly therapy is priced out of reach for a lot of the people who'd benefit from it most.

Door

Walking in is its own barrier

For social anxiety specifically, booking a call or sitting in a waiting room can be the hardest step in the whole process — before the help even starts.

Fit

Generic support doesn't stick

Most tools that are accessible aren't built for how ADHD and social anxiety actually show up day to day, so they get abandoned.

The loop

Knowing what to do was never the problem.

ADHD and social anxiety feed each other. Most CBT apps treat them as separate issues and hand you a blank page to figure it out on your own. That's exactly where the loop wins.

01

Starting feels impossible

Not laziness — a brain that struggles to initiate tasks without urgency or immediate reward. Job hunting, cleaning, admin all stall at the same point: the start.

02

Avoidance shrinks your options

A situation feels exposing, so you route around it — the job you don't apply for, the plans you skip. Each avoidance quietly narrows what feels possible next time.

03

Rejection sensitivity amplifies it

A small social slip gets read as proof something is wrong with you, which makes the next situation feel even riskier to approach.

04

Low motivation sets back in

Less exposure and more self-criticism drain the energy needed to start again — and the loop resets.
